## Purpose

This framework defines the standards and processes for managing technology vendors and contracts supporting the network infrastructure environment. Vendor and contract management is a governance function — not an administrative one. Poorly managed vendor relationships produce SLA failures, unplanned costs, security gaps, and operational risk.

This framework governs the full vendor lifecycle from selection through offboarding.

## Vendor Lifecycle

### Phase 1 — Selection and Due Diligence
- Define requirements aligned to architecture standards and security policy
- Evaluate minimum three vendors where market allows
- Assess vendor financial stability, support model, and reference customers in public sector
- Review cybersecurity posture: SOC 2, FedRAMP, or equivalent certification required for cloud-hosted services
- Confirm vendor alignment to City procurement and diversity requirements

### Phase 2 — Contract Execution
- All contracts must include defined SLA terms with financial remedies for non-performance
- Security requirements must be embedded in contract language — not left to vendor policy
- Data handling, breach notification, and incident response obligations must be explicitly defined
- Contract term, renewal options, and exit provisions must be documented before execution
- Legal and procurement review required before signature on all contracts above threshold

### Phase 3 — Active Management
- Assign a named contract owner for every active vendor relationship
- Conduct quarterly business reviews (QBR) for all Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors
- Track SLA performance monthly; escalate misses within 30 days
- Maintain a current vendor register with contract expiration dates, spend, and performance status
- Review vendor access rights semi-annually; remove access for inactive vendors immediately

### Phase 4 — Renewal and Renegotiation
- Begin renewal review no later than 180 days before contract expiration for Tier 1 vendors
- Begin renewal review no later than 90 days before expiration for all other vendors
- Benchmark pricing against market at every renewal
- Evaluate alternative vendors at every major renewal cycle
- Document renewal decision rationale regardless of outcome

### Phase 5 — Offboarding
- Revoke all vendor access within 24 hours of contract termination
- Recover all City-owned equipment and data within 30 days
- Conduct transition knowledge transfer before contract end date
- Document lessons learned for future procurement
- Retain contract records per City records retention policy

## Vendor Tiering

| Tier | Definition | Review Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 — Critical | Outage or failure causes immediate operational impact to City services | Quarterly QBR + monthly SLA review |
| Tier 2 — Essential | Failure causes degraded operations within 24–48 hours | Quarterly QBR + quarterly SLA review |
| Tier 3 — Standard | Failure manageable within normal change and procurement cycles | Annual review |

---

## SLA Standards

All network infrastructure vendor contracts must include SLA terms covering:

- Availability commitment (minimum 99.9% for Tier 1 services)
- Mean Time to Respond (MTTR) by severity level
- Mean Time to Repair (MTTR) by severity level
- Escalation path and named escalation contacts
- Financial remedies (credits or penalties) for SLA misses
- Reporting cadence and format

## Key Deliverables

| Deliverable | Description |
|---|---|
| Vendor Register | Current inventory of all active vendors with tier, contract expiration, and owner |
| SLA Performance Report | Monthly tracking of SLA compliance by vendor |
| QBR Summary | Documented outcomes from each quarterly business review |
| Contract Renewal Tracker | 180-day forward view of upcoming renewals requiring action |
| Vendor Risk Assessment | Annual review of vendor concentration risk and financial stability |

---

## Decision Criteria — When to Escalate

Escalate immediately when any of the following are identified:

- Tier 1 vendor misses SLA for two consecutive months without remediation plan
- Vendor access not revoked within 48 hours of contract termination
- Contract expiration discovered with less than 30 days remaining and no renewal in place
- Vendor security breach affecting City data or systems
- Single vendor providing more than 60% of critical network infrastructure with no alternative path
