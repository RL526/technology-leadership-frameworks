# Stakeholder Engagement and Executive Reporting Framework
**City of Philadelphia – Office of Innovation and Technology**

## Purpose

This framework defines the standards for stakeholder engagement, communication, and executive reporting across all network technology domains. Technical excellence without effective communication produces invisible value. This framework ensures that network leadership decisions, risks, investments, and performance are communicated clearly and consistently to the right audiences at the right cadence.

Effective stakeholder engagement builds trust, accelerates decision-making, and positions the network function as a strategic partner — not a back-office utility.

---

## Scope

| Audience | Included |
|---|---|
| CIO and technology leadership | Strategic reporting, risk escalation, investment recommendations |
| Agency technology liaisons | Operational updates, incident communication, project status |
| Budget and finance office | Capital planning inputs, cost reporting, ROI documentation |
| Procurement and legal | Vendor and contract management coordination |
| End users and agency staff | Service disruption notification, project awareness |
| External vendors and partners | Performance reviews, escalation management |

## Stakeholder Engagement Model

### Tier 1 — Executive Leadership (CIO, Deputy CIO, Mayor's Office)
- Engagement cadence: Monthly briefing + as-needed escalation
- Format: Executive dashboard — one page, visual, no jargon
- Content: Network health summary, active risks, investment status, upcoming decisions required
- Owner: DCNO

### Tier 2 — Technology Leadership Peers (CISO, CTO, Application Leaders)
- Engagement cadence: Bi-weekly operational sync
- Format: Working meeting with agenda and action items
- Content: Cross-functional dependencies, shared risk items, project coordination
- Owner: DCNO or designee

### Tier 3 — Agency Technology Liaisons
- Engagement cadence: Monthly operational update
- Format: Written report + optional call for high-impact agencies
- Content: Incident summary, project status at their sites, upcoming maintenance windows
- Owner: Network Operations team

### Tier 4 — End Users and Agency Staff
- Engagement cadence: As needed — incident notifications and project communications
- Format: Plain language email or service desk notification
- Content: What is happening, who is affected, when it will be resolved, what to do
- Owner: NOC or project team

## Executive Reporting Standards

### Monthly Network Health Report
Audience: CIO and technology leadership
Content must include:
- Network availability by tier (target vs. actual)
- P1 and P2 incident count, MTTR, and trend
- Top 3 active risks with mitigation status
- Capital project status summary
- Vendor SLA compliance summary
- One recommendation or decision request

Format: Maximum two pages. No raw data dumps. Every metric compared to prior month and target.

### Quarterly Executive Briefing
Audience: CIO, Deputy CIO, and budget leadership
Content must include:
- Quarterly performance scorecard
- Budget vs. actual spend with variance explanation
- Strategic initiative progress
- Emerging risks requiring leadership attention
- Forward look: next quarter priorities and resource needs

Format: Presentation deck — maximum eight slides. Executive summary slide required.

### Annual Technology Investment Report
Audience: CIO, Mayor's Office, budget office
Content must include:
- Year-over-year network performance comparison
- Capital investment summary and outcomes delivered
- EOL/EOS risk landscape and recommended refresh roadmap
- Three-year network investment forecast
- Strategic alignment narrative: how network investments support City priorities

## Communication Standards

- All executive communications reviewed by DCNO before distribution
- Incident notifications sent within 30 minutes of P1 declaration — no waiting for resolution
- Post-incident communications sent within 2 hours of P1 resolution
- No technical jargon in executive or agency-facing communications
- All reports use consistent templates — format changes require DCNO approval
- Action items from every stakeholder meeting documented and tracked to closure

---

## Decision Criteria — When to Escalate

Escalate immediately when any of the following occur:

- P1 incident affecting public-facing services — CIO notification required within 1 hour
- Any network risk assessed as High that does not have an active mitigation plan
- Capital project more than 30 days behind schedule or more than 10% over budget
- Vendor relationship breakdown that cannot be resolved at the DCNO level
- Any external inquiry (media, elected officials, oversight bodies) about network operations
